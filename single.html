In this paper, we show that by solving the app attribution problem we can automate the dynamic analysis of Android apps. We show this through developing a framework performing comprehensive step by step dynamic and automated teardown of the app registration and login procedures and their SSL endpoints. Using our app attribution solution to map network traffic to back to the apps and prevalent Android vulnerabilities spanning mishandled certificate validation, do-it-yourself cryptography, improper server configuration and other forms of information leakage, we were able to perform an automated man-in-the-middle attack and decrypt and monitor user traffic. Our analyses confirm earlier studies that 41% of Android apps are vulnerable to SSL/TLS man-in-the-middle attacks and 90% of them have at least have one major vulnerability potentially harming user confidentiality. Through this, we demonstrate that our automated system is an efficient and scalable solution for analysing Android apps dynamically.
